Gun Violence in America

By Darren, Laura, Lindsay, Kyu

Our Story:

Gun violence in America claims over tens of thousands of victims each year.
Compared to 22 other high-income nations, the U.S. gun-related murder rate is 25 times higher.
The United States' gun-related suicdie rate is eight times higher.
The United States has the most firearms per capita in the world.
Even though it has half the population of the other 22 high-income nations combined, the United States accounts for 82% of all gun deaeths.
Gun violence is a problem, and we want to use the following visualizations to show you just how bad it is.
But what good is a problem without a solution?
We also hope that through this site we can show you different factors that contribute to gun violence in America and what you can do to help.

Gun Violence and Mental Health

Below you'll find a graph based on available data of mass shootings from 1982-2020. Out of 118 incidents of mass shooting there was some kind of mental health data was available for 106 incidents. Even though almost half show no or unknown prior signs of mental disorder, many records show the perpetrator was angry, had mental illness in the family, had a history of drug and alcohol abuse, or had been described by neighbors, friends, or family as "troubled".
