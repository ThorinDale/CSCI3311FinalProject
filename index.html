Gun Violence in America

By Darren, Laura, Lindsay, Kyu

Our Story:

Gun violence in America claims over tens of thousands of victims each year.
Compared to 22 other high-income nations, the U.S. gun-related murder rate is 25 times higher.
The United States' gun-related suicdie rate is eight times higher.
The United States has the most firearms per capita in the world.
Even though it has half the population of the other 22 high-income nations combined, the United States accounts for 82% of all gun deaeths.
Gun violence is a problem, and we want to use the following visualizations to show you just how bad it is.
But what good is a problem without a solution?
We also hope that through this site we can show you different factors that contribute to gun violence in America and what you can do to help.
Keep scrolling to find out more...

Number of Firearm Laws by State over Time

The graph below shows the number of gun laws in each state over time. For most states, the number of gun laws have increased from 1990 to 2020. The states with the highest number of gun laws are mostly in the coastal regions of the country. The visualization also shows the types of gun laws that are the most popular in the country based on the year. For example, some states have gun laws that prohibit people with a history of alcoholism from acquiring firearms. Some states have gun laws that prohibit those with a criminal record from obtaining guns. Click on a state on the map to show the change in the number of gun laws from 1990 to 2020.

Gun Violence and Mental Health

Below you'll find a graph based on available data of mass shootings from 1982-2020. Out of 118 incidents of mass shooting there was some kind of mental health data was available for 106 incidents. Even though almost half show no or unknown prior signs of mental disorder, many records show the perpetrator was angry, had mental illness in the family, had a history of drug and alcohol abuse, or had been described by neighbors, friends, or family as "troubled". Click a bar in the bar graph below to show the related mass shooting incidents on the map to the right. The size of the points on the map represent the number of victims taken at each incident.
